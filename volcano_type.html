Three Main Volcano Types and Their Characteristics

Characteristics of a Shield Volcano

  • Very Large
  • Low Slopes
  • Lava Flows
  • Mafic Magma (fluid)
  • Effusive Explosions
  • Ex: Hawaiian Islands, U.S.

Characteristics of a Composite Volcano

  • Large and Symmetrical
  • Steep Slopes
  • Lava Flows and Pyroclastics
  • Felsic Magma (Viscous)
  • Catastrophic Explosions
  • Ex: Mt. Fuji, Japan

Characteristics of a Cinder Cone

  • Small and Symmetrical
  • Steep Slopes
  • Pyroclastics
  • Ex: Paricutin, Mexico
